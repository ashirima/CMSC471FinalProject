Why Does the Wealth Gap Exist?

Disparity in Higher Education

In many cases, higher paying professional careers are obtained by pursuing higher education. However, Black people generally have less access to higher education, which causes them to be underrepresented in higher paying fields. Below is a piechart that shows the breakdown of education attainment by race in adults aged 25 and older. Compared to their White and Asian counterparts, Black people are less likely to receive some sort of college degree.

Disparity in Incarceration Rates

Another cause of the diverging wealth gap is the incarceration rate of Black Americans compared to other races. This disproportional rate decreases the amount of working Black Americans, and consequentially, the amount of wealth Black Americans can hold. Each bar in the following graph represents the rate at which White Americans and Black Americans are incarcerated within each state. Click on each bar to view more details regarding the incarceration rates in each state.

Interestingly, the states with lower Black populations experience this disparity at higher levels compared to states with more Black Americans. This disparity keeps Black Americans that are already low in population from creating generational wealth because of lower employment rates and higher incarceration numbers. Impact of Systemic Disparities on Employment

Since less Black Americans are pursuing higher education and are incarcerated at higher rates, we see a trend in the types of jobs Black Americans work. The visual below describes the under and overrepresentation of Black, White, Asian, and Hispanic Americans at four income levels. These income levels represent the jobs that have a weekly income under the first quartile (Low), between the first quartile and median income (Mid-Low), between the median the the third quartile (Mid-High), and above the third quartile (High).

From this visual we can see that Black Americans are statistically more likely to be overrepresented in lower paying jobs and underrepresented in higher paying jobs. This could be a result of the lack of education needed for such jobs, or the impact of society steering Black Americans to more service oriented jobs.

The ratios for this data were calculated by dividing the race's representation in the specified jobs by the race's representation in the population. EX: If Black Americans made up 13% of the American population and 6% of the population of high-income jobs, this would calculate to 0.46. We then subtract that number from 1 which is our baseline and it gives us the ratio -0.53. Therefore Black Americans are underrepresented by a ratio of 0.53.
